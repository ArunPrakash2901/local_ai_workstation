One of the most underrated habits you could build in life is reading more books. Not because it makes you look smart, not because it's trendy, but because books give you access to something that most people never get, which is an experience without paying the full price for it. And so every book is someone who lived longer than you, made mistakes before you, went through them, failed before you, figured things out before you, and decided to write it down and share it with you. And that's decades of thinking, living, and learning compressed into a few hundred pages. Of course, reading isn't necessarily the same living experience yourself, but you won't feel everything the same way. You won't understand every lesson fully until you go through it and life tests you. But reading gives you awareness and awareness changes how you move through life. You start recognizing patterns earlier, decisions earlier. You avoid mistakes. You didn't need to make yourself. You learn how people think, not just what they do. And the biggest benefit that nobody talks about, reading slows you down. In this rapid, quick changing world, obsessed with speed. Books force patience, focus, and reflection. And so most people don't struggle because they're not capable. They struggle because they keep repeating the same damn mistakes. Mistakes someone already solved 50 years ago. And so you don't need to go through 50 books a year. You don't need to turn it into a competition. But if you read consistently, you quietly gain an edge that compounds over time in life because knowledge stacks, different perspectives stacks, wisdom stacks, and so read books. All right. Books won't change your life in a guaranteed way, but they won't live your life for you, right? But they'll help you live it with a fewer regrets.
