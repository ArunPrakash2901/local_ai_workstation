Would ICT unicorn model pass an institutional validation? I encoded this ICT model into a trading strategy which automatically identifies breaker blocks overlapping with a fair value gap. As the price retests this zone, we either open a short or a long position based if we are in the presence of either bearish or bullish fair value gap and breaker blocks. I applied this strategy on over 10 years of NQ data using a reward to risk ratio of two. And these are the results trading one NQ contract. A net profit of 41,000 over 10 years, a sloppy equity curve, and a low average trade profit of $13, which after commissions and slippage would result in a loss. Running our shuffling Monte Carlo, we can clearly see that the strategy is highly sequence dependent. And bootstrapping 20,000 equity curves, we get an expected value per trade of $9 with a confidence level between minus 44 and plus $62 per trade. No, the ICT unicorn model wouldn't pass an institutional validation. And this is not to attack or put shame on you because you use ICT. It is just to show you how valuable is how to test any strategy before trading it live, discovering if it works or not without losing any money.
