Let's go from zero to go trader in 30 days. We coded our entry logic. Now [music] we need to code our exit [singing] logic. And to be fair, we already started yesterday because in the prompt, we asked to close the position after 10 bars. The exact number of bars doesn't really matter right now because we will optimize it later. But there are two very important things I added in the prompt. One, to limit the strategy to only one trade per day. Otherwise, as long as the entry conditions remain valid, the strategy would keep opening positions. The second to close the position at the end of the session and this is crucial to keep the execution clean. If you don't do this, you risk to opening a trade late in the session and closing it in the next one which breaks our intraday logic. I always suggest to limit the very first version of each strategy to one trade per day. Why is that? It is much easier to debug and to verify the logic.
