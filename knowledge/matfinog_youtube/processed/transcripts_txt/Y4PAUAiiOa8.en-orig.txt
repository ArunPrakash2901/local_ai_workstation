Use charts for visualization, not decision making. With the adoption of computers on the trading floor, banks and hedge funds quickly realized that machines can analyze trends and price actions way more efficiently than humans. For years, the need to know how to code has been a massive barrier of entry, keeping retail traders stuck to look at charts the same way they did 30 years ago. Today, with the advent of large language models, that gap is finally shrinking. Your ability to develop, back test, and trade like an institutional trader comes down to one thing, your willingness to learn.
