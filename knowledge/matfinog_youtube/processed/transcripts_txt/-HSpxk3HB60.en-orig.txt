Quoting a wise guy, be fearful when the others are greedy. Be greedy when the others are fearful. The VIX volatility index aka the fear index measures the expected volatility of the next 30 days in the S&amp;P 500. It is called the fear index because generally spikes in period of fear and uncertainty. But rather than loading the boat in S&amp;P 500 leverage three times ETF as the VIX rises and selling when it relaxes, run a big test asking a simple question. What happens if a band sell the S&amp;P 500 at various VIX levels? Historically, it looks like buying as the VIX reaches levels between 40 and 55 and selling as it goes back between 14 and 16 is optimal. But this is not a financial advice. This is an example to push you to become a more aware trader. Leveraging the power of your computer to test your ideas and using your brain to understand the results.
