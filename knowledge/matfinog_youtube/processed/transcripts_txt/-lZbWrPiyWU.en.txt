In the last videos, I audited some of the biggest retail trading names in the industry. Today, I put Fabio Valentini's IVB model under institutional scrutiny. I coded Fabio Valentini's IVB model in a strategy which automatically identifies the initial trading balance and the opening price range. Then, when the price breaks that range and the move is confirmed by a strong delta, it opens a long position. I tested this strategy on 5 years of Nasdaq futures data using a 1:1 reward to risk ratio and trading one NQ contract. Here are the results. The strategy generated roughly $165,000. The equity curve is clear and upward sloping, and the average trade profit came in at around $200. The Monte Carlo shuffling shows a tight distribution around the mean, and bootstrapping 20,000 simulations, we get an expected value at around $194 per trade with a confidence interval ranging from $65 to $323 per trade. So, yes, Fabio Valentini's IVB model would pass an institutional validation. So, all you need to do is automating this system.
