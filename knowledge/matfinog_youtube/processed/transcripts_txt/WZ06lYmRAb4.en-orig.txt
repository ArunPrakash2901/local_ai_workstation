Everyone who has studied finance knows this model. It shows you the best combination of assets, the ideal market portfolio with the highest riskreward ratio. Then you move across the capital market line and adjust your leverage based on your risk capital. Higher the leverage, higher the expected return. What nobody told me at UNI is that in trading works very similarly, but instead of choosing the ideal asset portfolio, they choose the ideal strategy portfolio. Institutional traders treat each strategy as its own asset. They select the ones to run in production based on the combination that gives overall the best risk adjusted return.
